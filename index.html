About Me

Physics Educator with Research Passion

Hello! I'm Dr. Alex Morgan, a physics graduate with a deep passion for both teaching and research. After completing my doctoral studies in theoretical physics, I chose to bring my expertise to the high school classroom to inspire the next generation of scientists.

My teaching philosophy centers on inquiry-based learning and real-world applications. I believe that every student can understand and appreciate the beauty of physics when concepts are presented in an engaging and relevant manner.

While teaching full-time, I continue to contribute to the physics community through collaborative research projects and science communication initiatives.

Research Interests

Quantum Foundations

Exploring interpretations of quantum mechanics and their implications for education. Developing classroom demonstrations of quantum phenomena.

Astrophysics Education

Creating accessible curricula for high school students on cosmology and astrophysics. Researching effective visualization techniques for complex astronomical concepts.

Physics Education Research

Studying cognitive barriers in learning physics and developing evidence-based teaching strategies. Investigating the impact of computational modeling in physics classrooms.

Teaching Philosophy

Making Physics Accessible and Engaging

In my classroom, I emphasize the process of scientific inquiry over rote memorization. Students learn physics by doing physics - through hands-on experiments, computational modeling, and collaborative problem-solving.

I integrate modern technology and real-world applications to demonstrate the relevance of physics in everyday life and cutting-edge scientific advancements.

My approach has been recognized with the National Science Teaching Association's Outstanding Physics Teacher Award in 2023.
